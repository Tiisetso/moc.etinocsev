Pneumatic and Hydraulic Cylinder wear Components

Vesconite is a superior low friction polymeric material that is not affected by condensed moisture and oil making it ideally suited to hydraulic and pneumatic cylinder wear components.
Applications / Success Stories

Pneumatic and Hydraulic Fluid powercylinders need to guarantee a long service life.  The wear components in these cylinders therefore need to give the same long life performance.

What makes Vesconite well suited to long life components, such as wear rings and bearings?

Low Friction
Vesconite contains internal lubricants giving a low friction even if running dry. Oil lubrication further reduces the friction, and water is an excellent lubricant.

Dimensional Stability
  • Vesconite exhibits a negligible water or oil swell, which for practical purposes can be ignored.
  • Vesconite has a low thermal expansion rate in comparison to other polymeric materials. 
    (6.5 x 10-5     mm/mm/oC) (3.6 x 10-5  in/in/oF)

This allows designs with closer and more predictable tolerances.

High Load Carrying Capacity
 Vesconite has a compression strength of more than 80 MPa (11600 psi) and can safely be designed for static loads of 30 MPa (4250 psi). This is especially relevant for cylinder bearings.

These properties give

  • Very low wear rates to wearing components as well as mating surfaces.
  • Long component life expectancy.

Vesconite in comparison to other wearing materials :

Acetal (POM, Polyoxymethylene)
Vesconite gives a lower friction and a lower wear rate

Bronze
  • Vesconite is able to successfully operate in dry conditions.
  • Vesconite gives a lower friction and a lower wear rate in un lubricated conditions.
  • Vesconite gives excellent performance in wet conditions and is unaffected by condensate.

Nylons

  • Vesconite does not swell in water or oil and does not soften when immersed.
  • Vesconite has a higher load carrying capacity.
  • Vesconite has a lower wear rate.

UHMWPE

  • Vesconite has a higher load carrying capacity.
  • Vesconite has a much better dimensional stability.
