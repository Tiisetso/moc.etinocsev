Housings and Shafts

Housing and shaft tolerances and surface finishes.
Metal mating surfaces must be suited to the wear conditions encountered, for example when operating in sandy conditions. Hard shaft surfaces ensure longer shaft and bearing life. Designing bearings for dirty conditions required additional attention.

Hard chrome plated surfaces, seawater corrosion resistant stainless steel and admiralty and gunmetal bronze liners are generally satisfactory as mating surfaces for Vesconite.

Carbon steel and hardened steel shafts may be used as mating surfaces provided that they do not corrode under the conditions of use.

Metal tolerances
Standard steel tolerances can be used with Vesconite bearings.
Housings : H7
Shafts : h7

Surface finish
Housings
The surface finish on the housing is not critical as there is no movement after installation. For the purpose of installing the bearing the cut must not be too coarse. Lead in chamfers are required to ensure that the bearing does not score when been installed.

Shafts
The surface finish of the shaft is very important to ensure a correct operation of the bearing.
A good finish of the order of 2.5 µm Ra (20 micro inch Ra) should be provided on shaft surfaces for an optimum wear life. For shafts with an axial motion relative to the bearing, a finish of the order of 0.5 µm Ra (20 micro inch Ra) should be provided on shaft.
Rough surface finishes and corroded and scored shafts will cause wear of Vesconite bearings and should be avoided.
