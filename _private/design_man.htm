Vesconite Bush  Design Manual

Design and specify bushes that work better and cost less

What is Vesconite?
VESCONITE is a specialised thermoplastic made from internally lubricated polymers. It has been proven since the late 1960s to be an exceptional plain bearing material, giving long life in applications where only the lowest rates of wear can be accepted. Even when conditions are dirty and unlubricated, Vesconite gives long life together with low maintenance and low friction.

Vesconite Hilube is an advanced grade which combines the advantages of Vesconite with reduced friction and no stick-slip. It shows exceptionally low rates of wear in unlubricated and lubricated applications.

Vesconite is produced in what is probably the largest factory of its kind in the world, dedicated to the development, production and application of internally lubricated thermoplastic polyester bearing materials.

Advantages of Vesconite
Vesconite is extremely versatile. It outperforms most plain bearing materials in unlubricated, lubricated or water lubricated applications, in both clean and dirty conditions.

Vesconite combines many of the advantages of metallic and non-metallic bearings and bushes. It has a higher design load limit than white metal (babbitt), a higher fatigue strength and two to three times the wear life.

Vesconite gives up to 10 times the service life of phosphor bronze in poorly lubricated conditions.

Vesconite has negligible water absorption characteristics. Vesconite is one of the most dimensionally stable synthetic bearing materials available. Swelling due to moisture in the atmosphere or immersion, can lead to seizing but is avoided with Vesconite. This is a problem experienced particularly with nylon bearings unless excessive clearances are provided, which reduce wear life.

Vesconite has a much lower friction than most laminates, elastomers and nylons, and in most applications a superior resistance to wear. The low static and dynamic coefficients of friction of Vesconite mean less power loss. Users report greatly reduced wear rates on metal journal surfaces.

Vesconite is not susceptible to electrolytic corrosion.

Vesconite is resistant to most acids, solvents, hydrocarbons, oils and fuels.

Vesconite does not contain asbestos or other fibres. It is both easy and safe to machine on standard metalworking or woodworking machinery. Vesconite bushes are straightforward to install and easily removed.

New Drawings Needed
Frequently drawings for the previously used metal bushes are supplied when changing over to polymer bushes.

This is often inappropriate as the properties of polymers differ substantially from metals. The polymer part made according to the old drawing may give problems as well as being unnecessarily difficult to produce.

Polymer parts may also not conform to the quality control parameters set for metals, causing problems with quality assurance systems.

The purpose of this manual is to show how to design and specify Vesconite bushes for parts that will work better, last longer and cost less. The physical properties, design equations and tolerances discussed here apply to both Vesconite and Vesconite Hilube.

Differences Between Metals and Polymers
As background, it is useful to consider some of the basic differences between metals and polymers. These include thermal expansion, thermal conductivity, water absorption, compression strength and friction characteristics.

Thermal Expansion
Unreinforced polymers have thermal linear coefficients of expansion varying from 6 to 20 x 10-5 mm/mm/ 0C, compared with steel at 1,1 x 10-5 mm/mm/ 0C and phosphor bronze at 1,7 x 10-5 mm/mm/ 0C.

Vesconite has a thermal coefficient of 6 x 10-5 mm/mm/0C. This is particularly low for an unreinforced polymer, and makes Vesconite well suited to the production of medium precision bushes.

But this does mean that a temperature change of 20 0C alters a free standing Vesconite bush with a 100 mm diameter by 0,12 mm which must be allowed for. This will be unacceptable where a drawing specifies a tolerance of 0,025 mm, although feasible for a bronze bush expanding 0,02 mm under the same conditions.

A 100 mm diameter UHMWPE bush expands 0,4 mm over 20 0C!

Water Absorption
Unlike metals, most polymers absorb moisture from air or when immersed.

Nylon 6 has the highest moisture absorption, absorbing up to 9% by mass, causing a linear swell of 3% at saturation. Nylon 6 also absorbs around 3% of water at 65% relative humidity in ambient air, resulting in a 1% swell. This needs to be allowed for when designing nylon parts.

Vesconite has a very low level of water absorption: only 0,5% when saturated and 0,2% in ambient air. This results in a maximum swell of less than 0,05% which can generally be ignored.

Compression Strength
Unreinforced polymers usually have substantially lower hardness and compression strength than metals. Compression strengths vary from 11 MPa for PTFE (Teflon) to above 90 MPa for Vesconite.

One must also allow for creep phenomena. These increase with temperature and time, and require design limits to be substantially reduced from the ultimate compression strength at yield data.

Vesconite exhibits low creep properties, so that bushes can be designed up to a load limit of 30 MPa (300 kg/cm2) for static and slow speed applications. This compares with a 15 MPa load limit for thick white metal bushes.

It is however rare for bronze bushes to be designed for loadings over 30 MPa, as this loading exceeds the oil film strength.

Friction and Lubrication Requirements
Many polymer bushes offer friction coefficients much lower than metals when operating against a metal counterface without lubrication.

Polymer to metal friction coefficients vary from 0,01 to 0,30 depending on the polymer, load and speed. Unlubricated metal to metal friction coefficients range typically from 0,25 to 0,5. Metal to metal unlubricated sliding contacts often result in superficial seizures or "picking up".

The unlubricated coefficient of friction for Vesconite against metals ranges from 0,08 to 0,18. For this reason bushes made of polymers such as Vesconite are well suited to operate "dry" or with minimal lubrication.

Vesconite reduces the need for complex grease grooves and regular greasing. Initially lubricated operation also results in frictional coefficients much lower than those obtained with metal to metal designs.

Operation Under Water or Other Fluids
Metal against metal rubbing contact under water often leads to rapid wear. Vesconite operates well against metals under water or other fluids, provided that the metal counterface does not corrode. Vesconite can solve many of the design problems involved in immersed operations.

Press Fits
Polymers generally have a much lower rigidity than metals and show greater elasticity. This means that greater press (interference) fits and wider tolerances can be used.

The press fit recommended for normal conditions is:

Press fit = 0,05 mm + 0,2% housing diameter

Use greater press fits when operation below 50C is expected because Vesconite contracts on cooling. Your Vesconite distributor can help specify a suitable press fit.

Assembly Clearances
Metal bushes generally require fairly fine clearances to avoid noisy operation and possible brinelling. Polymer bushes on the other hand require medium to large clearances to allow for thermal expansion, thermal conductivity and water absorption.

Generally speaking, increased clearances do not cause problems with polymer bushes owing to their greater elasticity and resilience. Too fine clearances cause most of the failures associated with polymer bushes.

Vesconite bushes enjoy a relatively low thermal expansion coefficient and negligible water absorption. They may be used with clearances finer than those advised for most polymers. Nevertheless, clearances substantially larger than those specified for phosphor bronze bushes are generally required.

The clearance recommended for normal conditions is:

Temperature Effects on Bush Sizes
Size changes due to ambient temperature effects are relatively small for bushes that have been press fitted, but are large when the bush is free standing prior to fitting.

Ambient temperature changes affect the free standing dimensions of a Vesconite bush by 6 x 10-5 mm/mm/0C.

Wall thickness is the most important factor governing the operating clearance of a bush. For this reason, it is generally better to specify a fairly loose tolerance for the free standing dimensions, and a finer tolerance for the wall thickness because ambient temperature changes cause expansion and contraction of the inside and outside diameter of the free standing bush.

Alternatively the bush should be machined and checked at 20�C, which is more expensive to produce and less convenient to control.

Thermal Conductivity
Polymers have low rates of heat conduction compared with metals. This means that frictional heat is mainly dispersed through the metal shaft, rather than through the wall of the bush. As a result, the operating temperatures of polymer bushes tend to rise when running at high loads and speeds. This causes a loss of clearance which must be provided for.

Clearances for Higher Temperatures (50 - 100 0C)
If a press fitted bush will be used in a warm environment, or the temperature is likely to rise due to operation, then the clearance should be increased to allow for thermal expansion.

Stress Relaxation
This phenomenon affects polymers to a much greater extent than metals. It should be considered when designing press fitted bushes which will operate at high temperatures.

Stress relaxation occurs because many polymers lose elasticity between 70 and 100 0C. The press fit is lost at the higher temperature and the bush outside diameter tends to take on the size of the housing. When cooled, the bush contracts, leading to looseness in the housing, and possible closure on the shaft.

A "full float" design is advised in such cases. Your Vesconite distributor can assist in specifying your design.

Four Degrees of Precision for Vesconite Bushes
The higher the precision, the higher the cost. This applies especially to polymers which are more difficult to produce to the precision standards usually specified for metals.

Table 1 gives press fits, clearances and tolerances for four degrees of precision adopted for Vesconite. The client may choose the option best suited to his needs bearing in mind that higher precision involves higher costs.

High precision standards are often unnecessary as larger clearances are found suitable and appropriate to most applications.

For polymers, high precision specifications also require quality control to be carried out at a specific temperature. If this is not done, ambient temperature changes will lead to normal thermal expansion and contraction, leading to the wrong conclusions and to parts being rejected.

Specifying Wall Thickness
Because most bushes are press fitted into a housing, the free standing bush expansion or contraction causes a change only in the degree of press fit, without greatly affecting the bore diameter and assembled clearance.

For this reason, rather than specifying an outside and inside diameter to a close tolerance, for Standard Precision bushes it is better to specify the outside diameter to

� 0,1%, and control the wall thickness to a tolerance of +0,0/-0,5%.

Specifying the wall thickness and its tolerance also avoids the risk of the bush having a plus tolerance on the OD and a minus tolerance on the ID.

High Precision Bushes
If substantially finer tolerances are required for the dimen- sions of a free standing bush, High Precision bushes can be produced. These are machined with temperature compensation in relation to a Standard Ambient Tempera- ture of 20 0C. This increases machining costs. Quality control measurement checks will have to be carried out at 20 0C � 2 0C, which may not be convenient for the client.

Tolerances as fine as 0,01 mm can be attained by reaming the bush after press fitting it into the housing.

 Establishing the bush assembly clearance on the basis of a maximum clearance scenario may lead to unacceptably large clearances, while using a minimum clearance scenario may lead to seizure. Therefore use mean housing and shaft sizes on the assumption that it is unlikely that all the tolerances will occur in one direction only, but that averages are likely to apply.

Moulded Precision Bushes
Moulded Precision bushes are not machined. They are the most economical option when broad tolerances are acceptable. Moulded Precision bushes are generally only suitable for small and thin walled bushes.

Optional Design Features
Certain design features often specified for metal bushes can be dispensed with when using Vesconite bushes. This can effect substantial savings.

Chamfers
Generally metal bushes are specified with two chamfers on the outside diameter and two chamfers on the inside diameter, "edges broken", or with radii. Owing to the Vesconite bush being softer than its metal equivalents, only one external lead-in chamfer is required to lead the bush into its housing.

Grease and Oil Grooves
For metal bushes, complex grease grooves are often specified, such as "figure of 8" grooves. These are intended to avoid unlubricated areas within a metal bush.

Due to the greatly reduced lubrication needs of Vesconite, these grooves can often be left out or replaced by a simple straight or spiral groove acting as a grease reservoir.

In addition, grease nipples can often be dispensed with. The provision of "O" rings or felt washers will help to give a long life sealed "no maintenance" design.

Long life designs should incorporate a corrosion resistant shaft mating surface such as stainless steel or hardchromed steel. This is because a rusted surface will cause rapid damage to a Vesconite bush.

Surface Finishes
The Vesconite bush surface finish is not critical on account of its lower hardness.

The surface of the metal counterface is important for long bush service. We recommend polishing to 0,5 �m for optimum wear life, and the roughness should not exceed 2,5 �m. For solid-drawn shafts which have tool marks running in the axial direction, roughness should not exceed 0,5 �m.

Grooves and Seals
Increase in wear life by 3 to 10 times can be achieved under dirty conditions. Firstly, include straight grooves on the bush mating surface. These act as dirt removers and a store of grease. Secondly, use dirt excluding seals

("O" rings, felt seals) at both ends of the bush.

Grooves and seals are also well suited to "dry designs" when the longest possible life is required with or without initial lubrication at assembly.

"Non Normal" Conditions
This manual has been prepared to assist designers specify Vesconite bushes operating under normal conditions. For higher temperatures, loadings and speeds, or when unusual environments are encountered, complete a Vesconite Application Enquiry form and fax to your Vesconite distributor for an assessment of the application and a quotation.
