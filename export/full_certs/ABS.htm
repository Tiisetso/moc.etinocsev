This is to certify that Vesco Plastics

 has met the requirements of ABS Product Type Approval for

 Sleeve Marine, Rudder and Water Lubricated Propeller Shaft Bearings

Model Name(s): VESCONITE


Presented to:

Vesco Plastics
Artisan Street
P.O. Box 47

9430

Intended Service:
Water lubricated propeller shaft bearings. Strut on after stern tube and forward stern tube rudder stock bearings dry or water lubricated. Bushes fitted in housing, in or out of water.

Description:
Standard and Hilube unclad or with an outer polyester / epoxy composite cladding (Vesconite "Superclad"). Thermoplastic Polyester Polymer based busing for propeller shafts, rudder stock and pintle bearings. The outer diameter may be built up with Vesconite Superclad composite polyester reinforced epoxy resin to the required final outer diameter in a minimum composite thickness of 2 mm for bushings 100 mm outside diameter and upwards. The thickness of the Vesconite bush propeller will be in excess of 15 mm for grooved propeller shaft bearing and 10 mm for ungrooved rudder bearings.

Ratings:
Bearing surface pressures for rudder bearings up to 5.5 N/mm2 with the length / diameter ratio of the bearing surface not to be greater than 1.2. See note 4, below. The length of the bearing next to and supporting the propeller is not to be less than four times the required tail shaft diameter. Size range, physical and mechanical properties, friction and wear as per manufacturer's data.

Service Restrictions:
Basic design criteria for rudder bearings are maximum bearing pressure P not to exceed 5.5 N/mm squared, the length of bearing not to be greater than 1.2 times the required rudder stock diameter, and for the propeller support bearings, the length of bearing not to be less than 4.0 times the required tail shaft diameter. See also note a.) below. In addition, the environment is expected to be generally dry in a housing or in contact with water/sea water, at temperatures not exceeding 60 degrees C.


Comments:
a) Type approval of this type of bearing for propeller shafts and rudder stock bearings is based on compliance, in all respects, with ABS Rules in association with the intended service and is limited to the rating as indicated above; b) The bearing (sleeve bush bearing) is to be designed so as to ensure adequate water circulation and thus promote hydrodynamic lubrication; c) The running clearance, swelling of the material, wall thickness, machining allowance, interference fit of the bushing into a housing, installation procedure including locking of the bushes in the position within the housing are to be in accordance with manufacturer's recommendations; d) Higher values of the maximum allowable surface pressure for rudder bearing application than given in Table 3/5.2 of the Rules may be taken upon special application.

ABS Rules:
Building & Classing Steel Vessels Part 3, Sec. 5, Par. 3/5.13.1a Bearing Surfaces & 3/5.13.1b Bearing Clearance, Table 3/5.2 Bearing Pressure, Part 4 Sec. 7 Par. 4/7.25.1b Synthetic Bearing, Part 1 Sec. 3, Par. 1/3.13.3a Allowance Bearing Weardown
