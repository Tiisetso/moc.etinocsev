Is the constant greasing of bronze a hassle?
Solve the problem with self lubricating Vesconite.

Vesconite is better than bronze bushings.
Internally lubricated, longer life, no hassle.

Traditional bronze bushings must be greased to avoid rapid wear.

Vesconite requires little or no grease for long life

Less maintenance is required with Vesconite

Maintenance of machinery is costly and unreliable.
Manual greasing systems are often not used and automated greasing systems are expensive and lubrication lines may break off during operation.

Resources available for maintenance are being reduced and maintenance free systems are required.

Bronze bushings have a very high wear rate if there is no grease.

Machinery fitted with Vesconite bushings are more reliable and cost less. Vesconite can reduce or eliminate the need for greasing in many applications.

Vesconite survives even if greasing is stopped.

 

Low wear

Vesconite gives a low wear in poorly maintained applications and in wet and dirty conditions.

 

Dusty conditions

Exposed grease in dusty environments traps the dust causing a grinding paste that accelerates wear. This is especially a problem for linear bearings.
In dusty applications, Vesconite can operate without grease and eliminate the trapping of grease that results in high wear.
Vesconite is ideal for linear bearings.

 

Low friction

Internally lubricated Vesconite has a low friction even without grease. Vesconite without grease has half the friction of bronze that is greased.

Environmental and health concerns

Leaking grease damages the environment and contaminates the product. This is especially a concern in environmentally sensitive industries like Hydro Power, the food industry and the steel manufacturing industry.

Temperature extremes

Below freezing and high temperature applications make greasing difficult as greases change their characteristics at low and high temperatures. With Vesconite this problem can be solved in many applications.

Vesconite is ideal for sub-zero applications, even down to -40°C (-40°F)
